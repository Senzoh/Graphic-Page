🎨How I made The Poster🎨

Canvas size & Background🖼️

I started off by opening photoshop and selected a dimension size of 2480x3508. I then drew a circle on the screen using the marquee tool and filled the outside of the circle with a pink colour and the inner circle a dark navy. Next, I made new layer called “noise” with the same size circle and added static to this circle, by selecting filter > noise > add noise and changing the blend mode from “Normal” to “color dodge” along with moving the circle slightly to the top left giving it a 3D effect.

Statue🗿

To make the composite image I got a photograph of Michelangelo’s statue of David and using the quick selection tool, in another photoshop tab, to selected only the statue from the photograph. Next, I smoothed and feathered the selected area by going into select > select and mask, then I copy statue and pasted it into my main work and added a stroke transparent effect to it. I added a black rectangle over the statues genitals by drawing a rectangle using the marquee tool and filling it in black, also adding text over the eyes of the statue and adding a white stroke effect to the text.

Palm Tree silhouette🌴

I went through a similar process when adding the palm tree, however instead of copy and pasting the image of the palm tree, I decided to make it into a silhouette. I did this by using the paint bucket tool once the palm tree was selected and filling it in white. I then copied and pasted the white silhouette of a palm tree into my work and added the blend effect “Color Dodge” Which gave the palm tree a neon blue look.

Grid Floor🌐

To create the Pink floor grid, I made a new layer called grid and once again selected flirter then Vanishing point, from here I just drew out a grid. Next, I added two effects “color overlay”, which changed the colour of the grid and “outer glow”, which gave it a glow effect.

Blend Filter🎛️

Finally, to give the image an old vintage VHS look, I overlayed a black image of VHS distortion and added the blend mode “Screen”, this completed the image.
