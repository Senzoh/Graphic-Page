🎵Audio🎵

🎨How I made it🎨

Beach Waves.mp3🌊

To create the audio clip, I used the free audio editing software Audacity. This audio clip is made form 4 separate clips: “Beach Waves.mp3”, “Beer Can Opening.mp3”, “Seagull Call.mp3” and “Slurping.mp3”, all these clips being mp3 files. Once Audacity was open I Imported the first 20 seconds of the audio file “Beach Waves.mp3” , I then cut off 5 seconds off the end of the clip, but highlighting it and pressing the “del” key, I also reduced the sound clips volume by 27 decibel .

Seagull Call.mp3🐦

Next, I added the “Seagull Call.mp3” audio clip in a separate track. However, with this clip I used the whole thing, but I split the clip, by clicking where I wanted it to be split and pressed “ctrl+i”. After, to move the clip I selected the “time shift tool” and slid the second part of the audio clip more to the right. Since the clip ended so abruptly, I added a fade out effect by highlighting the part I wanted to be faded and pressed effect>fade out.

Beer Can Opening.mp3🍺

Then I imported the “Beer Can Opening.mp3” audio file and trimmed 3 seconds of empty noise at the end and reduced the volume by 15 decibels to make the sounds more immersive to the previous sounds. When playing back the audio I noticed that the clip contained a little bit of static noise, to get around this I highlighted a section with just the static and went to effect>noise reduction and clicked “get noise profile”, I then applied that to the whole clip.

Slurping.mp3🥤

Finally, the last clip I included was “Slurping.mp3”. originally the audio file included various slurping sounds, however I picked one which I felt sounded appropriate for my audio and removed all the other ones, next I moved it into place and reduced the volume by 10 decibels, so that the sounds was not overpowering.
